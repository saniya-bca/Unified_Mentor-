2. How to Start Meal Prepping

Step 1: Plan Your Meals

- Decide how many meals you need per week (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks).
- Include a balance of protein, complex carbs, healthy fats, and vegetables.
- Choose meals that store well in the fridge or freezer.

Step 2: Make a Grocery List

- Stick to the plan and avoid impulse buys.
- Buy ingredients that can be used across multiple meals.

Step 3: Cook in Batches

- Prepare proteins (chicken, tofu, lentils) together.
- Roast or steam vegetables in bulk.
- Cook grains like rice, quinoa, or pasta in large portions.

Step 4: Store Properly

- Use air-tight containers or mason jars.
- Label containers with meal name and date.
- Refrigerate meals for up to 4 days, or freeze for longer storage.

3. Healthy Meal Prep Ideas

Breakfast

- Oats: Mix oats, milk, chia seeds, and fruits in a jar. Refrigerate overnight.
- Egg Muffins: Beat eggs with vegetables, pour into muffin tins, bake, and store.
- Smoothie Packs: Pre-measure fruits and greens in freezer bags. Blend with milk or yogurt in the morning.

Lunch

- Quinoa & Veggie Bowls: Quinoa, roasted veggies, chickpeas, and a light dressing.
- Grilled Chicken Salad: Grilled chicken, mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, and avocado.
- Veggie Wraps: Whole wheat wraps with hummus, spinach, cucumber, and shredded carrots.

Dinner

- Baked Salmon & Veggies: Salmon fillets with roasted broccoli, zucchini, and sweet potato.
- Stir-Fry: Mixed vegetables with tofu or lean meat, served with brown rice.
- Lentil Soup: Protein-rich soup with lentils, carrots, celery, and spices.

Snacks

- Nuts and seeds
- Greek yogurt with berries
- Cut vegetables with hummus
- Rice cakes with peanut butter
